The Real Cost of Email Overload

Every time you open your email client, your brain has to context-switch. Research from the University of California Irvine found it takes an average of 23 minutes to fully regain focus after an interruption. Multiply that by 74 daily email checks and you're not working anymore — you're just restarting over and over.

The good news: most of this is fixable. The bad news: willpower alone won't fix it. You need a system.

Habit #1: Checking Email First Thing in the Morning

Opening your inbox before you've done any focused work is like letting someone else write your to-do list. You immediately shift from creating to reacting.

The fix: Block your first 60–90 minutes for deep work. Let SaneBox quietly sort everything overnight so when you do open your inbox, only genuinely important emails are waiting.

Newsletters, CC'd threads, automated notifications — none of these are urgent. But when they sit in your inbox alongside a client email, your brain processes them with equal weight.

The fix: SaneBox's SaneLater folder automatically routes low-priority messages out of view. You can review them in a batch at the end of the day in under 5 minutes.
